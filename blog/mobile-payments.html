Foreigners & Mobile Payments in China: A Complete 2025 Guide

Published March 2026 • 8 min read

In 2026, China has moved even further into its cashless revolution. For international visitors, understanding the mobile payment landscape isn't just a matter of convenience—it's essential for everything from buying street food to booking high-speed rail tickets.

The Big Two: Alipay & WeChat Pay

While cash is still legally accepted, its use is becoming increasingly rare. The two dominant players are Alipay (by Ant Group) and WeChat Pay (within the WeChat app by Tencent). In the last two years, both platforms have made massive strides in supporting foreign credit cards.

Setting Up Before You Land

The single biggest mistake travellers make is waiting until they arrive in China to set up their accounts. Here is our recommended checklist:

  • Download both apps: While they are similar, having a backup is crucial.
  • Verify your identity: You will need to upload a photo of your passport. This process can take anywhere from a few hours to two days.
  • Link multiple cards: Visa, Mastercard, and Discover are now widely supported. We recommend linking at least two different cards in case one triggers a bank security block.

Common Hurdles for Foreigners

Even with "support," foreigners often run into issues like transaction limits or verification failures during peak hours. Some smaller merchants might only support "personal transfers," which occasionally don't work with linked international cards.
