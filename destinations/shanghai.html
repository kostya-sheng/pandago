Shanghai

Experience the Pearl of the Orient

A Vision of Tomorrow

Shanghai is China's most cosmopolitan city, a place where European colonial history and hyper-modern ambition collide. The Bund, with its majestic neoclassical buildings, looks across the Huangpu River to the soaring skyscrapers of Lujiazui, representing the dramatic transformation of the city over the last century.

From the luxury boutiques of Nanjing Road to the hidden coffee shops of the former French Concession, Shanghai is a city that never stops moving. It is the perfect entry point for those seeking a blend of international comfort and authentic Chinese culture.

Best Time to Visit Spring (March–May) & Late Autumn (Oct–Nov)
Must Eat Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings), Hairy Crab
Skyline View Shanghai Tower (Highest in China)
Shanghai Highlights

The Bund

A mile-long promenade along the Huangpu River, the Bund is the architectural heart of old Shanghai. Its 52 buildings of various architectural styles (Gothic, Baroque, Romanesque, Art Deco) offer a glimpse into the city's role as a 19th-century financial hub.

French Concession

Characterised by tree-lined avenues and European-style architecture, this area is home to fashionable boutiques, art galleries, and some of the city's best cafes. It offers a quieter, more romantic side of the city.

Lujiazui Skyline

Home to the Oriental Pearl Tower, the Jin Mao Tower, and the Shanghai Tower, this district is the financial engine of the city and offers some of the most spectacular observation decks in the world.
