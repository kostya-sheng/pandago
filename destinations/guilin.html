Guilin

Experience Nature's Masterpiece

Scenery That Inspired Poets for Millennia

Guilin is world-renowned for its ethereal karst mountain landscapes and the winding Li River. It's often said that "Guilin's scenery is the best among all under heaven," and one look at the mist-covered peaks at sunrise is enough to understand why.

From the terraced rice fields of Longji to the bamboo raft rides in Yangshuo, Guilin offers a serene escape into the natural beauty of southern China. It is the perfect destination for hikers, photographers, and anyone looking to slow down.

Best Time to Visit April–October (Li River is fullest)
Must Eat Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen), Beer Fish
Top Experience Li River Cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo
Guilin Highlights

Li River Cruise

The 83-kilometre stretch between Guilin and Yangshuo is the centerpiece of any trip. You'll pass dramatic peaks, grazing water buffalo, and fishermen on traditional bamboo rafts—including the famous spot featured on the 20 Yuan note.

Longji Rice Terraces

Located about two hours from Guilin, these "Dragon's Backbone" terraces offer stunning views of mountain slopes carved into intricate steps. They are particularly beautiful during the spring flooding or the autumn harvest.

Yangshuo

While Guilin is the city base, Yangshuo is where the real magic happens. Surrounded by karst peaks, it's a hub for outdoor activities like rock climbing, cycling, and exploring the West Street night market.
