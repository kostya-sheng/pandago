Beijing

Experience the Capital

A City Where History Meets the Future

Beijing is a city of layers, where the ancient echoes of the Forbidden City and the Temple of Heaven stand alongside futuristic skyscrapers and the buzzing energy of modern commerce. As the capital of China for over 800 years, it remains the political and cultural heart of the nation.

Whether you're hiking the rugged Great Wall at Mutianyu or exploring the narrow hutong alleys where traditional life still unfolds, Beijing offers an unparalleled deep dive into Chinese heritage.

Beijing Highlights

The Great Wall

Stretching thousands of miles across northern China, the Great Wall is a testament to human ingenuity. Near Beijing, sections like Mutianyu and Jinshanling offer breathtaking views and a direct link to the Ming Dynasty's defensive architecture.

The Forbidden City

The world's largest palace complex, this imperial citadel served as the home of 24 emperors. Its 9,999 rooms are filled with treasures, intricate artwork, and centuries of imperial history.

Hutong Exploration

To see the "real" Beijing, one must enter the hutongs—narrow alleyways formed by lines of traditional courtyard residences. It's here that the city's pulse beats strongest, in the street food stalls and quiet courtyards.
