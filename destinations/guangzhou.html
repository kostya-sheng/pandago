Guangzhou

Culinary Capital of China

A Hub of History and Gastronomy

Guangzhou, also known as Canton, is a city where tradition and modernity blend seamlessly along the Pearl River. Famous for its world-class Dim Sum and vibrant markets, it's a paradise for food lovers and culture seekers alike.

From the iconic Canton Tower to the historic European-style architecture on Shamian Island, Guangzhou offers a unique perspective on China's Southern charm and global trade history.

Best Time to Visit Autumn (Oct–Dec) & Spring (Mar–May)
Must Eat Dim Sum, Cantonese Roasted Meat
Food Heritage The Birthplace of Cantonese Cuisine
Guangzhou Highlights

Canton Tower

An architectural marvel and one of the tallest towers in the world, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city skyline and the Pearl River.

Shamian Island

A serene, pedestrian-only island featuring beautifully preserved European-style colonial buildings, quiet streets, and lush banyan trees.

Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

A masterpiece of traditional Cantonese architecture, known for its intricate wood, brick, and stone carvings, as well as its beautiful folk art museum.
